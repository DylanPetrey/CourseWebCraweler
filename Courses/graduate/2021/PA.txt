PA 6347 Capital Budgeting and Debt Administration (3 semester credit hours) The course offers an in-depth overview of the decisions and processes involved in purchasing capital assets by U.S local governments and other entities that issue capital debt for public purposes. It covers topics related to the management of capital expenditures including the creation of capital budgets, the debt issuance process, options in structuring capital debt, and issues involved in the management of capital improvements or rehabilitation of physical assets to extend their useful life. The course provides students with hands-on experience of creating and analyzing debt service schedules and concludes with an applied project. Prerequisite: EPPS 6313. (3-0) R

PA 8330 Inside Washington: Policymaking from the Ground Up (3 semester credit hours) This course competitively selects a group of UT System graduate students to spend an 11-week summer program in Washington, D.C. The course is designed to complement students' experiences at their accompanying three-semester credit hour internship placement. The course provides a context for and a familiarity with the dynamics that influence all activity in the government, including meeting officials from the White House, House and Senate, nonprofits, lobbying firms, think tanks, the media, and others. Students return with a participant's understanding of the workings of the remarkable machinery of the federal government. Corequisites: PA 8331 and PA 8332 and instructor consent required. (3-0) Y

PA 8332 Archer Center Washington Internship (3 semester credit hours) Students are competitively selected to participate in an 11-week summer program in Washington, D.C. The internship experience provides students with the opportunity not only to view the activity of the government, but to participate in it as well. The internships, which are monitored by the Archer Center, integrate the student into the work of the office or agency, treating the intern like a junior staff member. Students thus participate in and come to understand the nature, the pace, the context, and the intensity of federal government policy making. The internship is complemented by a required 3-semester credit hour, academic course. Pass/Fail only. Corequisites: PA 8330 and PA 8331 and instructor consent required. (3-0) Y
